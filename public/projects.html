PROJECTS COMPLETED BY FUTURES OF ART
 

WE DELIVER WONDERMENT, INSPIRATION AND NEW USES OF OLD AND EMERGING TECHNOLOGIES
Curiosity and the ability to see into the near future allow us to deliver seamless technology experiences led by artists who may/may not have any technical skillsets. We teach to understand what is possible and take it from there.
 

what do we do

WITH AN AVERAGE OF 25,000 VISITORS PER EXHIBITION, WE AIM TO INSPIRE EVERYONE.
Some of recent exhibitions have involved 500 drones over Circular Quay in Sydney or augmenting a hospital to bring more art to medical teams, we have been busy with new work coming out soon.

In the meantime here are a few things we have been up to:
 

CENTRE POMPIDOU

EXPLORING NEW REALITIES
Delivering all augmented reality activations as part of 'Cosmopolis #2 - rethinking the human' at the Centre Pompidou in Paris and in collaboration with artists Lisa Reihana, Tricky Walsh and Nandita Kumar.

BIENNALE OF SYDNEY

REAL TIME LANGUAGE TRANSLATION
In consultation with the Mimili Maku Arts, Brook Andrew and the Art Gallery of NSW, we used Augmented Reality to translate the installation in the Main Court of the gallery so audiences can access the deeper meaning behind each of the 36 banners.

BUBBLE EDUCATION

CO-VID 19 SAFETY FOR KIDS
Working with Apple Education and St Therese Catholic Primary School, we created a 360 Augmented Reality environment for new kindergarten students.

JAPAN
SUPER-NATURAL

EXHIBITION LAUNCH WOW
With over 25,000 interactions, the collaboration with artist Kentaro Yoshida was an opening night success at the largest exhibition of Japanese art in Australia.
